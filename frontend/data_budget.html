📚 Data Types Guide (Click to expand)

▼
Telemetry: Real-time health and status data from subsystems (e.g., temperatures, currents).
Image: Visual data captured by cameras or sensors (e.g., Earth observation).
Science Data: Raw measurements from mission-specific sensors or instruments.
Video: High-bandwidth visual streams for observation or recording.
Housekeeping: Background data tracking the satellite's long-term health and history.
GPS/Nav: Positional data, velocity, and time synchronization from global navigation systems.
Telemetry and Commands: Bidirectional signals for the CDHS to report status and receive operational directives.
TT&C: Telemetry, Tracking, and Command. Critical digital signals linking the satellite with ground control (health, ranging, and uplink commands).

⚠️ NOTE: Meas/Min stands for Measurement/min (How many data points are collected every minute)
